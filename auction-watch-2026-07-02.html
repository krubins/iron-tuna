Buy Jonathon Brooks before the price catches up

Jonathon Brooks was the standout of Panthers offseason work, and his draft price has climbed about a round and a half in the past month as drafters catch on. He is finally healthy after two ACL tears, and some analysts now project him to lead the Carolina backfield outright. The window to get him at a discount is closing. Bid with conviction now, and do not pay Chuba Hubbard's old price for a back losing work.

Rachaad White is a PPR buy in Washington

Rachaad White signed a one year, two million dollar deal with the Commanders and reunites with Jayden Daniels, his college teammate at Arizona State. Washington rebuilt its backfield with Jacory Croskey-Merritt positioned for early down work, leaving White the receiving role. Familiarity with a young quarterback tends to mean check down volume. Pay a few PPR dollars for the receptions, but do not bid him up as a weekly starter.

Discount Malik Nabers until the knee news clears

Malik Nabers underwent a second procedure on his surgically repaired right knee to remove scar tissue, and league reporting puts his Week 1 availability in real doubt even as Giants management stays publicly optimistic. The talent is not the question; the timeline is. Do not pay a healthy WR1 auction price. Bid only if the room lets him fall a full tier below his usual cost.

Keep waiting on Alvin Kamara

New Orleans is still working out how Alvin Kamara fits alongside Travis Etienne, and his ADP has kept dipping as the uncertainty drags on. The endgame value case remains, but there is no reason to bid early. Let the room forget about him, then take him at a clear discount as a late PPR piece with a defined receiving role.

Bucky Irving anchors a crowded Tampa backfield

The Buccaneers kept a three man committee by signing Kenneth Gainwell and bringing back Sean Tucker alongside Bucky Irving. Irving remains the clear anchor and the only Tampa back worth real auction money, but the depth behind him caps his touch ceiling. Pay for his volume as a solid RB2, and stop short of a true bell cow bid.
