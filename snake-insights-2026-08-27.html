The Ravens lost hidden support structure on offense

Baltimore’s former scheme used multiple backs and TEs heavily; then the class lost Patrick Ricard and Isaiah Likely and saw Tyler Linderbaum leave for Las Vegas.

The play: Treat as tier-dependent and avoid forcing the pick. Still draft stars, but do not overproject Ravens role players.

Projected effect: slight efficiency drag on Lamar/Henry/Andrews relative to 2025 peak usage

02
UnderpricedQBMedium confidence

Matthew Stafford is expensive for a non-rushing QB, but the case is real

He was QB3 in 2025 at 21.1 FPPG and threw 46 TDs, with Puka and Davante already proven together.

The play: Move the player or group up roughly one round, or to the front of the current tier. Fine in best ball, slightly less appealing in managed leagues because of the closing schedule.

Projected effect: +0% to +4% versus price in season-long, but lower playoff comfort

03
Near fair valueTEMedium confidence

Mark Andrews remains touchdown-reliant

He projects 7.7 TDs, which is high, but Baltimore’s ecosystem lost pieces.

The play: Draft at market slot only; do not move up unless the tier is about to dry up. Better in TE-premium and TD-heavy formats than standard PPR.

Projected effect: fair in standard, slightly weak in full PPR

04
Near fair valueRBMedium confidence

Barkley’s target environment can improve post-A.J. Brown

Trade, but overall offense likely gets less efficient.

The play: Draft at market slot only; do not move up unless the tier is about to dry up. Neutral overall, but less exciting than his name value suggests.

Projected effect: small reception boost, modest touchdown-rate decline

05
UnderpricedWRMedium confidence

Deebo Samuel in Washington is still a scheme-created weekly flex, not a true target king

He was WR25 in 2025, and Washington added multiple backfield pieces plus Okonkwo.

The play: Move the player or group up roughly one round, or to the front of the current tier. Better in best ball.

Projected effect: boom/bust flex rather than dependable WR2
