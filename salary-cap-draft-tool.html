Salary cap drafts reward discipline, not nerve

In a salary cap, or auction, draft, you are not picking in turn, you are allocating a fixed budget across a full roster. Every dollar you overspend on one player is a dollar missing from another slot. The managers who win are not the ones who land the biggest name, they are the ones whose last roster spot still has real points in it. A salary cap draft tool's whole job is to protect that last dollar.

Spend the whole cap, on purpose

Iron Tuna builds a budget-aware plan that turns your cap into the strongest legal starting lineup, then shows you the dollar allocation behind it. You can see, before the draft, roughly how much should go to your top three starters versus depth, and you can flex that with a stars-and-scrubs or balanced slider. During the draft, the plan re-solves after every purchase so you never strand money you should have spent or overcommit early and get priced out of a starter.

  • Every player priced to your exact cap, roster, and scoring
  • Live tracking of money off the board and each team's remaining cap
  • Position scarcity, so you know where the cheap points are still hiding
  • A reserve for your final roster spots, so you are never forced into $0 dead slots

Read the room's cap, not just yours

The other managers' budgets are public information you can use. When two teams are out of money, the remaining players go cheap, and a disciplined manager pounces. Iron Tuna tracks every team's remaining cap so you can see those value windows opening before the rest of the table notices, and time your nominations to drain rivals while wallets are still full.

FAQ
Is a salary cap draft the same as an auction?

Effectively yes. You allocate a fixed budget across a full roster by bidding, rather than picking in turn. Iron Tuna is built for exactly this format.

Can it handle any cap size?

Yes. Set your total budget and roster, and every player is priced to your cap, whether you play $200, $300, or something custom.

How does it stop me from overspending early?

The budget-aware plan reserves money for your remaining slots and re-solves after every purchase, so you are never forced into empty $0 roster spots.
