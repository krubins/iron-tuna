A.J. Brown gives Drake Maye a true No. 1

The Patriots traded for A.J. Brown, pairing him with third year quarterback Drake Maye and reuniting him with head coach Mike Vrabel. Brown profiles as a low end WR1 with a clear target spike coming, so pay up with confidence rather than letting his post Philadelphia down year scare you off.

Wait on Aaron Rodgers in Pittsburgh

Aaron Rodgers signed with the Steelers and reunited with Mike McCarthy, and his ADP has jumped roughly two rounds on the news. At his age the ceiling is capped, so do not pay up. Treat quarterback as a position to wait on and let someone else overbid for the name.

Kenneth Walker III is Kansas City's lead back

Super Bowl LX MVP Kenneth Walker III left Seattle and signed a three year deal with the Chiefs as their clear lead back. In a strong Kansas City offense he carries RB1 upside on early down and goal line work, so be willing to pay up for the projected workload.

Bhayshul Tuten leads the Jacksonville backfield

Travis Etienne signed with the Saints, opening the door for second year back Bhayshul Tuten in Jacksonville. Tuten sits atop the depth chart, though veteran Chris Rodriguez Jr. is expected to take touches, so target Tuten as the lead while respecting the committee risk on his price.

Josh Downs is the volume beneficiary in Indianapolis

With Michael Pittman Jr. traded to Pittsburgh, the Colts vacated 111 targets. Josh Downs is the most likely beneficiary of that underneath and intermediate work and projects near 100 targets, making him a value riser to target in the middle rounds before the market adjusts.
