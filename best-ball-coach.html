What the coach does

The Best Ball Coach turns your draft into a guided experience. As you pick, it tracks your position counts against the optimal build for best ball, flags when you fall behind, and surfaces ceiling-ranked players tailored to your team. It also watches for stacks as they form and surfaces championship-week targets separately so you can intentionally weight your late picks toward players who face soft defenses in Weeks 15–17.

Your build tracker

The "Your Build" section shows your current position counts at a glance: how many quarterbacks, running backs, wide receivers, and tight ends you have versus the targets for an 18–20 round draft. If you're behind on a position, the coach calls it out and prioritizes recommendations there for the next few picks, so you finish building the right shape before the market moves. The message "Build is on pace" means you can trust ceiling and take the best available; "behind on WR" means the next two picks should lean toward receiver to fill your quota.

Draft next

The "Draft Next — Built for Your Roster" section shows the five highest-ceiling players ranked for your specific team. This is not a generic board; it accounts for the positions you need, the stacks you've already built, and whether you're ahead or behind on the roster shape. Take these in order when positions are close and you are unsure, or break out to the ceiling board when you have room to chase upside.

Stacks on the board

The coach surfaces unfinished stacks as you draft. If you pick a quarterback, any of his pass-catchers still available light up here. If you pick a receiver, his quarterback lights up. These are opportunities to amplify a stud you already own: draft a quarterback with one or two of his own receivers and one big game from that offense booms multiple roster spots in the same week — the correlated ceiling that wins best ball.

Championship-week edge

While general ceiling ranking is first, the "Championship-week Edge" section pulls out players with the softest defenses during Weeks 15–17. Best ball titles are won in those three weeks, not in September. By intentionally stocking your bench with players who peak late, you ensure your roster ceiling is highest exactly when the title is on the line. The coach surfaces these separately so you can make the choice: chase general ceiling, or build for the finish.

Common questions

What is the Best Ball Coach?

A live draft assistant built into the best ball tool that tracks your build, flags stacks as they form, and recommends the highest-ceiling players for your specific roster. It guides every pick without replacing your judgment.

How do I use the Build Tracker?

The Build Tracker shows your position counts. When you're on pace, take the best ceiling available. When you're behind on a position, the coach prioritizes recommendations there until you hit your target. When you're ahead, you have room to chase elite players or stacks.

Why does the coach rank different players for my team than a generic board?

Because context matters. The coach knows your stacks, your position needs, and whether you're building for ceiling or filling a hole. A wide receiver is more valuable to a team that just drafted his quarterback than to one that isn't stacking him.

Should I always follow the "Draft Next" recommendations?

Not always — use it as a tiebreaker when two players are close in ceiling for your team. If you see a player you like outside the top five recommendations, feel free to reach. The coach is a guide, not a straitjacket.
